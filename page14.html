Project 3: Daydream Video Project

Artist Statement

I set out to create a video that encapsulated two things I love: filmmaking and retro video games. The idea of a dreamscape went well with these concepts, and I chose to insert myself into two classics: Super Mario Bros and Pulp Fiction. As you can imagine, Tarantino's film was a lot trickier to work with, as I had to remove the original actor who gets shot in the scene with Samuel L. Jackson and John Travolta. With some clever VFX, I pulled it off and added myself in with a chroma key. I opted to lip sync over redubbing anything to save time and sell the effect a bit better.

The Mario scenes were a bit easier, albeit tedious. I created some sprites of myself matching the 8-bit style of SMB1 and looped them in a walking animation. I also intercut with jumping animations and other effects. I attempted to remove Mario from the original footage, but decided it wasn’t worth the immense effort of tracking him and creating a fill. I enjoy the end result as it can be viewed like I’m running alongside Mario. 
The SFX for this video we’re too complicated, but certainly involved. Most you won’t notice, but I did redo all the sounds from SMB1 with creative commons tracks. I chose not to create or add in any music for most of the video, as I didn’t believe it would live up to the source material, and my number one goal was to show authenticity.
